Hi! I'm Braden Dredge.

Fun facts about me:

  • I'm a member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints! Formerly a ward choir pianist, deacon's quorum president, teacher's quorum president, priest's quorum first assistant, full-time missionary (Guatemala Guatemala City East, Spanish-speaking! 2017-2019), ward pianist, and elder's quorum secretary. I currently serve as a ward mission leader. And of course, one of the MOST IMPORTANT covenants I have ever made was the one I made with my amazing and beautiful wife Kerena, with whom I was sealed in the Provo City Center Temple in November 2021!
  • I'm completing two bachelors degrees at BYU (Provo, UT) -- one in Economics, and another in Computer Science. I am also completing minors in Design Thinking and Entrepreneurship.
  • I'm a musician! My work can be found on my YouTube Channel, or on my SoundCloud.

    "White Withered Rose" (vocals, guitar) is a song I wrote back when I was 2014, around the time I moved to Oregon. "Suburban Seasons" is a composition (oboe, piano, and strings) I made back in 2015, inspired by the feelings I felt about the neighborhood I moved into. "Heavenly Father Has a Plan" was a song (vocal, piano, violin) I wrote on my mission for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. "Infant Holy, Infant Lowly" is a piano arrangement I made for Sacrament Meeting after returning from my mission.

  • I'm a total math geek AND nerd. I am a big fan of Numberphile and OneBlueThreeBrown videos on YouTube. I was so excited about my curvy cup measurement project back in high school calculus that I literally emailed my middle school teacher to tell him about it (I was so proud of it). This is why I chose to study two math-heavy fields, and I aspire to do lots of math in my career.
  • You're reading the bio of a spelling bee champ. Yep. I participated in the California Nationwide Spelling Bee back in my prime. A bit of a grammar Nazi.
  • I'm also a geek for languages. I've started about 4 different languages on Duolingo (Spanish, German, Portuguese, French), and every now and then I try to learn more from each. I also try to find Spotify playlists to listen to so that I get some more exposure.
